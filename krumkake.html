Krumkake

Description:

Krumkake, a beloved delicacy of Norwegian origin, embodies the essence of Scandinavian sweetness and tradition. This delicate, lacy, and lightly crispy pastry is a cherished part of Norwegian culinary heritage, often enjoyed during festive occasions, celebrations, and holiday gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Vanilla extract
  • Cardamom (optional)
  • Your grandma's hand-me-down krumkake iron, guarantied to give you at minimum a second-degree burn

Preparation

  1. Prepare the batter by blending flour, sugar, eggs, melted butter, and a hint of vanilla extract until smooth. Optionally, add a touch of cardamom for a delightful Nordic flavor.
  2. Heat a specialized krumkake iron over a stovetop or electric burner until hot.
  3. Spoon a small amount of batter onto the iron, close it, and allow the batter to cook until golden brown and lightly crispy.
  4. Remove the delicate krumkake from the iron and carefully shape it into a cone or cylinder while it's still warm and pliable.
  5. Let the krumkake cool and firm up before serving. Optionally, dust with powdered sugar for an extra touch of sweetness.
